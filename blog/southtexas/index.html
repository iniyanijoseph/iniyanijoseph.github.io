South Texas Trip

McKinney → McAllen → Corpus Christi

Late June Road Trip Itinerary

Departs: Craig Ranch, McKinney, TX — Tue, 9:00 AM
Returns: McKinney — Sun evening,  8:30 PM
Weather: Sunny / hot, 90-95°F+ daily

Dietary plan

1 vegan, 2 low-carb, 1 no dairy/soy.

Meals default to Mexican / Tex-Mex or Mediterranean spots with mixed menus (never vegan-only restaurants) — rice, beans, fajita veggies, guacamole, salsa, hummus, and falafel are naturally vegan; everyone customizes from there (skip cheese/sour cream for dairy-free, swap rice/tortillas for extra veg or protein for low-carb). Dinners are planned a little later in the day, eaten back near the hotel, except where a must-see spot includes a dinner stop.

Day 1 — Drive Day (Tuesday)

McKinney → McAllen

9:00 AMDepart Craig Ranch, McKinney
 11:30 AMSan Antonio River Walk — lunch + stretch,  1.5-2 hrs
 6:30 PMArrive McAllen, check in
7:30 PMDinner near hotel — Tex-Mex, build-your-own plates

Tip:  7.5 hr total drive with the River Walk stop. Try La Hacienda de los Barrios for lunch — it has a dedicated vegetarian section plus customizable fajita / rice / bean plates that work for everyone.

Day 2 — McAllen, Hiking & Nature (Wednesday)

8:00 AMBentsen-Rio Grande Valley State Park (Mission,  25 min away) — 7 miles of trails, observation tower. Go early, before the heat.
11:30 AMMcAllen Nature Center — 2 miles of Tamaulipan thornscrub trails, free admission ( 1 hr)
1:00 PMLunch in McAllen — Tex-Mex, build-your-own plates
2:30 PMQuinta Mazatlan — historic adobe estate house tour + birding grounds ( 1 hr)
3:45 PMIMAS (International Museum of Art & Science) — fully indoor, A/C ( 2 hrs)
6:00 PMDowntime / freshen up at hotel
7:30 PMDinner at home

Tip: Bentsen Park is the only outdoor walk on this day — doing it first thing keeps the group out of the worst midday heat. Quinta Mazatlan and IMAS both offer shade/A.C. relief through the hottest hours.

Day 3 — Drive Day + Arrival (Thursday)

McAllen → Rockport via Kingsville

10:00 AMDepart McAllen
 11:30 AM1904 Train Depot Museum (Kingsville) — Local railroad history brought to life by volunteer docents, including an operational telegraph. Kingsville was built around this rail line.  45 min.
12:30 PMDrive to Rockport ( 1.5 hrs)
 2:00 PMFulton Mansion State Historic Site — 1877 Victorian mansion on Aransas Bay. Last admission 3:15 PM; open until 4 PM.  1 hr.
 3:15 PMRockport Center for the Arts — Four galleries + sculpture garden, one block from Aransas Bay. Free. Open until 4 PM Thu.  45 min.
 4:00 PMTexas Maritime Museum — Gulf Coast history from the Karankawa people through Spanish explorers, commercial fishing, and offshore oil. Free. Observation deck with bay views. Open until 4 PM Thu.  1 hr.
 5:00 PMHarbor stroll — Rockport Harbor and piers. Boats, pelicans, bay breeze.
6:30 PMDinner — Pier 77 — Top-rated Rockport waterfront spot. Seafood-forward with salads and flexible sides. Confirm menu for dietary needs before going.

Tip: Kingsville is the natural midpoint and far more interesting than a generic lunch stop. The Fulton Mansion is right near the hotel in Rockport; if the timing doesn’t work on arrival day, it can swap with something from Days 4–5.

Day 4 — Aquarium, North Beach, T-Head Pier (Friday)

10:00 AMTexas State Aquarium ( 2.5 hrs) — book tickets online ahead
1:00 PMQuick snack / break — lighter midday bite, dinner is later tonight
1:30 PMNorth Beach — free time ( 2.5 hrs): swim, shells, Gulf breeze
4:00 PMPeoples Street T-Head & CC Marina ( 1.5 hrs) — pier over the bay, moored sailboats, pelicans
7:30 PMDinner — Ginger Cafe & Grill

Tip: Ginger Cafe & Grill (Mediterranean) covers all four diets in one stop: hummus, falafel, and dolmas for vegan; kabobs and salads for low-carb; olive-oil-forward dishes for dairy-free. Menu is clearly marked for vegan and gluten-free items. The T-Head is a 10-min drive from North Beach — walk to the end of the pier for dolphins and pelicans up close.

Day 5 — Culture + Padre Island Beach (Saturday)

10:00 AMCC Museum of Science & History ( 2 hrs) — shipwrecks, local history, planetarium
12:00 PMHeritage Park — free, walkable 19th-century Victorian homes
1:00 PMLunch — Zambra Fusion Cafe
2:45 PMSelena Memorial Statue & Mirador de la Flor — free, open 24/7
4:00 PMPadre Island Seawall — Beach Block ( 3 hrs) — open Gulf beach, chair/canopy rentals on site, great for sunset
7:30 PMDinner at home

Tip: Zambra Fusion Cafe puts vegan, vegetarian, and regular dishes side by side on one menu — including an Impossible-burger swap — so nobody eats at a “vegan only” spot. Padre Island faces the open Gulf — better waves and shells than North Beach; canopy + chairs rent for about $65 on site.

Flag: Double-check Art Museum of South Texas hours for Saturday specifically — it was originally scheduled assuming a Sunday 1 PM opening. If hours don’t align, it can be dropped or the order adjusted. The Selena Museum (Mon–Fri only) no longer fits this trip’s weekday window — worth dropping unless it’s a must-see, in which case the schedule would need rework.

Day 6 — Botanical Gardens, Final Beach, Depart (Sunday)

9:00 AMSouth Texas Botanical Gardens & Nature Center ( 1.5 hrs) — orchids, butterfly house, exotic birds
11:00 AMPadre Island — final beach session ( 1.5 hrs)
1:00 PMLunch in Corpus Christi before hitting the road
2:30 PMHead home —  6 hr drive
 8:30 PMArrive back in McKinney
