McKinney → McAllen → Corpus Christi

Late June Road Trip Itinerary

Departs: Craig Ranch, McKinney, TX — Tue, 9:00 AM
Returns: McKinney — Sun evening, ~8:30 PM
Weather: Sunny / hot, 90-95°F+ daily

Dietary plan

1 vegan, 2 low-carb, 1 no dairy/soy.

Meals default to Mexican / Tex-Mex or Mediterranean spots with mixed menus (never vegan-only restaurants) — rice, beans, fajita veggies, guacamole, salsa, hummus, and falafel are naturally vegan; everyone customizes from there (skip cheese/sour cream for dairy-free, swap rice/tortillas for extra veg or protein for low-carb). Dinners are planned a little later in the day, eaten back near the hotel, except where a must-see spot includes a dinner stop.

Day 1 — Drive Day (Tuesday)

McKinney → McAllen

9:00 AM Depart Craig Ranch, McKinney
~11:30 AM San Antonio River Walk — lunch + stretch, ~1.5-2 hrs
~6:30 PM Arrive McAllen, check in
7:30 PM Dinner near hotel — Tex-Mex, build-your-own plates

Tip: ~7.5 hr total drive with the River Walk stop. Try La Hacienda de los Barrios for lunch — it has a dedicated vegetarian section plus customizable fajita / rice / bean plates that work for everyone.

Day 2 — McAllen, Hiking & Nature (Wednesday)

8:00 AM Bentsen-Rio Grande Valley State Park (Mission, ~25 min away) — 7 miles of trails, observation tower. Go early, before the heat.
11:30 AM McAllen Nature Center — 2 miles of Tamaulipan thornscrub trails, free admission (~1 hr)
1:00 PM Lunch in McAllen — Tex-Mex, build-your-own plates
2:30 PM Quinta Mazatlan — historic adobe estate house tour + birding grounds (~1 hr)
3:45 PM IMAS (International Museum of Art & Science) — fully indoor, A/C (~2 hrs)
6:00 PM Downtime / freshen up at hotel
7:30 PM Dinner at home

Tip: Bentsen Park is the only outdoor walk on this day — doing it first thing keeps the group out of the worst midday heat. Quinta Mazatlan and IMAS both offer shade/A.C. relief through the hottest hours.

Day 3 — Drive Day + Arrival (Thursday)

McAllen → Rockport via Kingsville

10:00 AM Depart McAllen
~11:30 AM 1904 Train Depot Museum (Kingsville) — Local railroad history brought to life by volunteer docents, including an operational telegraph. Kingsville was built around this rail line. ~45 min.
12:30 PM Drive to Rockport (~1.5 hrs)
~2:00 PM Fulton Mansion State Historic Site — 1877 Victorian mansion on Aransas Bay. Last admission 3:15 PM; open until 4 PM. ~1 hr.
~3:15 PM Rockport Center for the Arts — Four galleries + sculpture garden, one block from Aransas Bay. Free. Open until 4 PM Thu. ~45 min.
~4:00 PM Texas Maritime Museum — Gulf Coast history from the Karankawa people through Spanish explorers, commercial fishing, and offshore oil. Free. Observation deck with bay views. Open until 4 PM Thu. ~1 hr.
~5:00 PM Harbor stroll — Rockport Harbor and piers. Boats, pelicans, bay breeze.
6:30 PM Dinner — Pier 77 — Top-rated Rockport waterfront spot. Seafood-forward with salads and flexible sides. Confirm menu for dietary needs before going.

Tip: Kingsville is the natural midpoint and far more interesting than a generic lunch stop. The Fulton Mansion is right near the hotel in Rockport; if the timing doesn’t work on arrival day, it can swap with something from Days 4–5.

Day 4 — Aquarium, North Beach, T-Head Pier (Friday)

10:00 AM Texas State Aquarium (~2.5 hrs) — book tickets online ahead
1:00 PM Quick snack / break — lighter midday bite, dinner is later tonight
1:30 PM North Beach — free time (~2.5 hrs): swim, shells, Gulf breeze
4:00 PM Peoples Street T-Head & CC Marina (~1.5 hrs) — pier over the bay, moored sailboats, pelicans
7:30 PM Dinner — Ginger Cafe & Grill

Tip: Ginger Cafe & Grill (Mediterranean) covers all four diets in one stop: hummus, falafel, and dolmas for vegan; kabobs and salads for low-carb; olive-oil-forward dishes for dairy-free. Menu is clearly marked for vegan and gluten-free items. The T-Head is a 10-min drive from North Beach — walk to the end of the pier for dolphins and pelicans up close.

Day 5 — Culture + Padre Island Beach (Saturday)

10:00 AM CC Museum of Science & History (~2 hrs) — shipwrecks, local history, planetarium
12:00 PM Heritage Park — free, walkable 19th-century Victorian homes
1:00 PM Lunch — Zambra Fusion Cafe
2:45 PM Selena Memorial Statue & Mirador de la Flor — free, open 24/7
4:00 PM Padre Island Seawall — Beach Block (~3 hrs) — open Gulf beach, chair/canopy rentals on site, great for sunset
7:30 PM Dinner at home

Tip: Zambra Fusion Cafe puts vegan, vegetarian, and regular dishes side by side on one menu — including an Impossible-burger swap — so nobody eats at a “vegan only” spot. Padre Island faces the open Gulf — better waves and shells than North Beach; canopy + chairs rent for about $65 on site.

Flag: Double-check Art Museum of South Texas hours for Saturday specifically — it was originally scheduled assuming a Sunday 1 PM opening. If hours don’t align, it can be dropped or the order adjusted. The Selena Museum (Mon–Fri only) no longer fits this trip’s weekday window — worth dropping unless it’s a must-see, in which case the schedule would need rework.

Day 6 — Botanical Gardens, Final Beach, Depart (Sunday)

9:00 AM South Texas Botanical Gardens & Nature Center (~1.5 hrs) — orchids, butterfly house, exotic birds
11:00 AM Padre Island — final beach session (~1.5 hrs)
1:00 PM Lunch in Corpus Christi before hitting the road
2:30 PM Head home — ~6 hr drive
~8:30 PM Arrive back in McKinney

Practical Notes

  • Heat: Upper 90s°F+ daily expected. Hit outdoor activities in the morning or after 5 PM when possible. Sunscreen, hats, and water are non-negotiable.
  • Dietary heads-up: This corridor is seafood- and meat-heavy. The spots listed above all flex well across vegan / low-carb / dairy-free / soy-free, but it’s worth a quick menu check ahead for anywhere not on this list.
  • Beach parking: Padre Island charges a small annual pass fee (about $12) to drive on the sand. Street/lot parking is free.
  • Texas State Aquarium: Book tickets online — summer Saturdays fill up fast.
  • McAllen heat strategy: Day 2′s outdoor hiking is front-loaded to the morning; indoor/shaded culture stops fill the hot afternoon.

Day 1

Today was a pretty straightforward day. We began around 8:15. I wanted to leave earlier, but as always things got delayed. We took the slightly longer path through highway 281 through Central Texas and the hill country. It was really beautiful, even if I was the only one who appreciated it. I ate quite a bit, especially cheese and fruit, but I am hoping to burn enough calories in the next few days that it doesn’t do much. I can see why eating healthy is so difficult in McAllen.
