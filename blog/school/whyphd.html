As I begin a PhD in Theory CS, I have received questions about what made my want to do a PhD, especially in a theory field. I typically answer these questions separately.

In computer science, a PhD is the first step towards an academic career. Research is one of the most important ways to make real societal impact. The impacts of research apply beyond my immediate circles and community, and farther into the future than my own life. I also love teaching, and I believe it has a valuable role in society. In the words of Victor Hugo in Les Miserables: “[Monsieur Madeleine] declared that the future lies in the hand of the schoolmaster… He desired that society should labor without relaxation at the elevation of the moral and intellectual level, at coining science, at putting ideas into circulation, at increasing the mind in youthful persons.” A PhD gives the opportunity to teach directly as a TA, and the opportunity to teach afterwords if you choose an academic career.

As for why Theory CS, there are three reasons. From a purely cuios perspective, I find it interesting mathematically, additionally, the rigor of the field challenges me and pushes me to learn and think. Beyond curiosity, it has direct applications to society. The applications of niches like fair division and economics and computation immediately improve society, but even otherwise it has other practical application in a world where more data is being processed. With theory especially, the applications may extend beyond the obvious immediate applications and help build up the structure to solve future problems.
