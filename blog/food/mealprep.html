Component 1 — Challah Loaf

Makes 14 servings

Ingredients

  • 180g warm water
  • 50g sugar
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 500g bread flour
  • 80g vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 6g salt

Optional: sesame seeds

Method

  1. Combine water, sugar, yeast.
  2. Rest 5 min.
  3. Add flour, oil, eggs, yolk, salt.
  4. Knead 8–10 min.
  5. Rise 90 min.
  6. Braid loaf.
  7. Rise 45 min.
  8. Brush with egg white.
  9. Bake at 175C for 25 min.

Portioning

Slice into 14 portions of 90g. Freeze slices individually.

Component 2 — Roasted Vegetable Mix

Makes 10 portions

Ingredients

  • 1200g mushrooms
  • 800g onion
  • 800g bell pepper
  • 40g olive oil
  • 10g salt

Method

  1. Slice vegetables.
  2. Toss with oil + salt.
  3. Roast at 220C for 30–35 min.
  4. Stir halfway through.
  5. Cool completely.

Portioning

Portion into 10 containers of 280g. Freeze.

Component 3 — Spinach Base

Makes 10 portions

Ingredients

  • 2000g frozen spinach
  • 25g garlic
  • 15g olive oil
  • 8g salt

Method

  1. Heat oil.
  2. Cook garlic 1 min.
  3. Add spinach.
  4. Cook until excess moisture evaporates.

Portioning

Portion into 10 containers of 180g. Freeze.

Component 4 — Tomato Lentil Base

Makes 8 portions

Ingredients

  • 350g dry lentils
  • 1000g canned tomatoes
  • 300g onion
  • 20g garlic
  • 15g olive oil
  • 8g salt

Method

  1. Cook lentils until tender.
  2. Sauté onion in oil.
  3. Add garlic.
  4. Add tomatoes.
  5. Simmer 15 min.
  6. Stir in lentils.

Portioning

Portion into 8 containers of 250g. Freeze.

Component 7 — Paneer Cubes (2500 grams)

Makes 14 portions

Ingredients

  • 3¼ gallons (12.5 liters) whole milk (full-fat, non-ultra-pasteurized preferred)
  • ¾ cup lemon juice (or distilled white vinegar)
  • Salt to taste

Method

Stage 1: Heat Milk

  1. Pour milk into pot.
  2. Heat slowly to 86°F (30°C), stirring occasionally to ensure even heating.
  3. Once at temperature, maintain steady heat for 30 minutes.
  4. Slowly add lemon juice while stirring continuously with circular motions.
  5. Line colander with double layer of cheesecloth.
  6. Pour curds and whey through cheesecloth.
  7. Gather cheesecloth and squeeze out excess whey.
  8. Place bundle in mold lined with muslin cloth.
  9. Fold muslin over top.
  10. Place weight on top (5–10 lbs).
  11. Press for 30–45 minutes at room temperature.
  12. Unwrap and cut into cubes or desired shape.

Portioning

Portion into 14 containers of 180g. Freeze.

Component 8 — Pizza Dough Balls

Makes 6 pizzas

Ingredients

  • 630g bread flour
  • 15g sugar
  • 10g salt
  • 10g instant yeast
  • 32g olive oil
  • 420g water

Method

  1. Combine dry ingredients.
  2. Add water + oil.
  3. Knead until smooth.
  4. Divide into 6 dough balls ( 185g).
  5. Refrigerate 1–5 days.

Can also freeze after 24h fermentation.

Meal 8 — High-Protein Pasta Salad (Meal Prep Version)

750 calories | cold or warm | very high satiety

This is your:

  • “no effort dinner”
  • “backup meal”
  • “easy lunch swap”

Ingredients (1 serving)

  • 90g dry pasta
  • 160g cooked pinto beans
  • 140g paneer
  • 200g roasted vegetable mix
  • 120g spinach base
  • 15g olive oil
  • 20g balsamic vinegar
  • 10g salt (to taste)

Optional:

  • chili flakes
  • garlic powder
  • lemon juice (5–10g)

Method (Assembly Only)

  1. Cook pasta (or batch cook and refrigerate).
  2. Reheat roasted vegetables + spinach base (optional, also fine cold).

  3. Add:

    • pasta
    • beans
    • paneer
    • vegetables

  4. Toss with:

    • olive oil
    • balsamic vinegar
  5. Season and mix well.

Texture Options

Cold (pasta salad style):higher convenience, slightly firmer texture Warm bowl: higher satiety, more aromatic
